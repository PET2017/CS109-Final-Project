The Fluctuations and Effects of Housing Prices

An analysis by Jordan Rogers, Eric Rodrigo, and Alex Harding

Background:

Housing prices are key indicators to the status of the United States economy. The upward and downward changes in prices correlate to the business cycle of the economy. The 2008 housing crises goes to show just how important the housing market is on the economy. Since housing prices are a key indicator of the status quo of the economy, it is important to see which factors affect housing prices such as income, crime, and race. It is also interesting to see how housing prices vary across the nation and over time.

Objectives:

Through this project we sought to answer four key questions:

  1. How do key factors such as age, race, vacancy rate, population, crime, and metropolitan status affect housing prices?
  2. How do housing prices vary over time and location?
  3. What effects did the 2008 financial crisis have on housing prices across the nation?
  4. How have housing prices fluctuated over time?
